## PHASE 3 - THE STRATEGIC & PSYCHOLOGICAL ENGINE

### Phase 2 Success Criteria

By the end of this phase, the learner can:

- defend technical and product decisions in calm, natural B2 English,
  even when meetings are tense or cross-cultural
- explain failure, uncertainty, motivation, focus, and responsibility
  with more depth than standard interview answers
- connect psychology, business reality, and engineering trade-offs
  without sounding abstract or academic
- give answers that include one clear tension, one practical example,
  and one honest limitation or trade-off
- handle a final 30-15 mock format:
  30 minutes of hard questions, then 15 minutes of open depth discussion

### Session 10 - Defending Decisions Across Teams

- Focus: defend decisions across different communication cultures while staying calm under pressure.
- In scope: cultural intelligence, tone adaptation, Stoic calm, cost-benefit language,
  and handling sharp pushback from stakeholders.
- Out of scope: general uncertainty frameworks and post-incident trust repair.
- Required output: 1 cross-cultural decision memo + 1 tense meeting dialogue + 1 calm pushback script.
- Practical knowledge targets: directness vs hierarchy, face-saving language,
  reversal cost, and business-facing trade-off explanation.

### Session 11 - Owning Mistakes & Rebuilding Trust

- Focus: explain failure without blame and rebuild trust through psychological safety.
- In scope: incident accountability, trust recovery, blameless learning,
  and humility through software crisis case references.
- Out of scope: broad bullying or exclusion handling.
- Required output: 1 failure narrative + 1 trust-rebuild message + 1 prevention plan.
- Practical knowledge targets: psychological safety language,
  responsibility without defensiveness, and incident learning structure.

### Session 12 - Deep Work, Distraction & Focus Recovery

- Focus: protect deep work by understanding attention, dopamine loops, and recovery rhythm.
- In scope: attention economy, flow, circadian rhythm, focus design,
  and NSDR-style recovery habits in a realistic engineer schedule.
- Out of scope: long-term burnout recovery planning.
- Required output: 1 focus-system map + 1 distraction audit + 1 weekly deep-work routine.
- Practical knowledge targets: cognitive load language,
  rhythm-aware scheduling, and defending focus time professionally.

### Session 13 - Burnout Recovery & Sustainable Energy

- Focus: distinguish short-term fatigue from systemic burnout and build sustainable energy.
- In scope: overload signals, recovery science, sustainable output,
  and practical health systems for a fast-moving industry.
- Out of scope: identity questions and general attention-control routines.
- Required output: 1 energy recovery protocol + 1 weekly burnout-risk review + 3 boundary lines.
- Practical knowledge targets: sustainable pace language,
  energy diagnostics, and recovery behaviors that are practical rather than dramatic.

### Session 14 - Making Decisions with Incomplete Data

- Focus: make better choices when evidence is incomplete and incentives are messy.
- In scope: behavioral economics, nudges, intuition vs logic,
  reversible decisions, downside control, and AI-era product judgment.
- Out of scope: defending a decision after the team has already aligned on a direction.
- Required output: 1 uncertainty decision map + 1 nudge-based product example + 1 spoken answer.
- Practical knowledge targets: choice architecture,
  downside framing, bias awareness, and practical decision language.

### Session 15 - Motivation, Meaning & Skills That Last

- Focus: understand what keeps a career meaningful when tools and stacks change fast.
- In scope: ikigai, adaptability quotient, motivation shifts,
  durable skills, and long-term work meaning.
- Out of scope: compensation negotiation and family pressure scripts.
- Required output: 1 values map + 1 skills longevity reflection + 1 personal meaning answer.
- Practical knowledge targets: transferable strengths,
  adaptability language, and honest motivation beyond role title.

### Session 16 - Ownership, Initiative & Working Trust

- Focus: build trust and influence by filling important gaps without waiting for authority.
- In scope: social capital, remote or hybrid trust, initiative,
  business value awareness, and human connection inside delivery work.
- Out of scope: generic motivation reflection and salary negotiation.
- Required output: 1 ownership story + 1 social-capital action map + 1 cross-team support script.
- Practical knowledge targets: relational trust,
  initiative with boundaries, and value creation beyond formal scope.

### Session 17 - Responsible Engineering & Hidden Cost

- Focus: connect engineering choices to global resources, privacy standards, and digital ethics.
- In scope: green code, sustainability, historical and cultural awareness,
  compute responsibility, and practical ethical contribution from small code decisions.
- Out of scope: abstract climate policy debate without engineering action.
- Required output: 1 green engineering checklist + 1 ethical trade-off note + 1 spoken reflection.
- Practical knowledge targets: sustainability language,
  responsibility framing, and linking small technical choices to wider impact.

### Session 18 - Final Mock: Think Clearly Under Pressure

- Focus: final 30-15 format with pressure handling first, then deeper open discussion.
- In scope: difficult follow-ups, structured speaking, reflective thinking,
  and open discussion on topics such as Big Tech pressure or algorithm ethics.
- Out of scope: a separate baseline mock before the final round.
- Required output: 1 final scorecard + 1 depth discussion review + 1 post-course plan.
- Practical knowledge targets: pressure recovery,
  topic flexibility, and finishing strong with thoughtful, natural answers.

- Session 11 != Session 13: trust after failure vs energy recovery and burnout prevention.
- Session 12 != Session 13: focus control vs sustainable energy and burnout prevention.
- Session 16 != Session 15: initiative and working trust vs meaning, values, and skill longevity.
- Session 17 != Session 07: global ethics and sustainability vs product privacy and trust design.
- Session 18 != old Session 25: one final integrated mock with depth discussion, not a two-mock sequence.
