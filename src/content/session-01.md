# BUỔI 1: Your Role & Work (Simple)

**Level:** B1+
**Main role:** Senior Frontend Developer (5 years experience)

## 1) Vocabulary (along with 2-3 useful + typical examples)

### responsibility (n): trách nhiệm

- I take full responsibility for the entire user interface.
- One of my key responsibilities is to ensure the application is responsive on all devices.

### collaborate (v): hợp tác

- I collaborate closely with backend developers and UI/UX designers.
- We collaborate every day through Jira and daily stand-up meetings.

### maintain (v): duy trì, bảo trì

- I maintain and improve the existing frontend codebase.
- We regularly maintain the system to keep it fast and secure.

### optimize (v): tối ưu hóa

- I optimize web performance to reduce loading time.
- My goal is to optimize both speed and user experience.

### user experience (UX) (n): trải nghiệm người dùng

- Good frontend development directly improves user experience.
- We focus heavily on delivering a smooth and intuitive user experience.

### cross-functional team (n): đội ngũ đa chức năng

- I work in a cross-functional team that includes frontend, backend, QA, and product owners.

## 2) Collocations, Chunking & Phrasal Verbs (rất hay dùng khi nói)

### Collocations

- take responsibility for
- work closely with
- deliver high-quality
- ensure smooth user experience
- handle daily tasks
- improve system performance
- bridge the gap between design and code
- meet business requirements

### Chunking & Useful Phrases

- On a daily basis, I…
- My main role is to…
- I’ve been responsible for…
- One of the most important parts of my job is…
- I’m in charge of…
- From a technical perspective…
- In terms of user experience…

### Phrasal Verbs (rất thực tế)

- **look after** → I look after the frontend part of the project.
- **work on** → I work on multiple features at the same time.
- **deal with** → I often deal with performance issues and browser compatibility.
- **come up with** → I come up with solutions to improve the UI.
- **carry out** → I carry out code reviews with junior developers.

## 3) Typical Dialogues (thực tế + có objection handling)

### Dialogue 1 – Basic Introduction

**Teacher:** So, tell me about your job. What do you do?
**You:**
I’m a senior frontend developer with 5 years of experience. My main role is to build and maintain the user-facing part of our web application.

On a daily basis, I work closely with designers to turn Figma designs into clean, responsive code. I also collaborate with backend developers to integrate APIs and make sure everything works smoothly. Besides coding, I sometimes do code reviews and help junior developers in the team.

### Dialogue 2 – With Objection (thực tế khi nói với manager/non-tech)

**Teacher:** Honestly, isn’t frontend just about making things look pretty?
**You:**
Actually, it’s much more than that. While design is important, my job is to make sure the application is not only beautiful but also fast, accessible, and easy to use on any device.

For example, last quarter I optimized the checkout process. After that, the page loading time dropped from 4.5 seconds to 1.2 seconds, and our conversion rate increased by 18%. So good frontend work directly affects business revenue and customer satisfaction.

### Dialogue 3 – Short & Natural

**Teacher:** Who do you usually work with?
**You:** I work in a cross-functional team. I collaborate closely with UI/UX designers, backend engineers, QA testers, and the product manager. We use Agile methodology, so we have daily stand-ups and sprint planning together.

## 4) Reading Text (ngắn, dễ hiểu, thực tế)

I have been working as a senior frontend developer for five years. My main responsibility is to create the visual interface that users see and interact with every day. I don’t only focus on making the application look good — I also make sure it is fast, responsive, and provides a smooth user experience across different devices and browsers.

I work in a cross-functional team where I collaborate with designers, backend developers, and product owners. Every day, I work on new features, fix bugs, optimize performance, and sometimes help improve the development process. My work directly impacts how users feel when they use the product, which ultimately affects customer satisfaction and the company’s business results.

## 5) List of Questions + Ideas (thực hành đa dạng)

### Basic Questions

1. What is your current job title?
2. How long have you been working as a frontend developer?
3. What are your main responsibilities?
4. What does a typical workday look like for you?

### Deeper & Follow-up Questions

5. Who do you collaborate with the most in your team?
6. What kind of tasks do you handle on a daily basis?
7. What do you like most about your job?
8. What is the most challenging part of being a frontend developer?
9. How does your work affect the end users?
10. In what ways does frontend development contribute to business goals?

### Objection / Discussion Questions (practice defending your role)

11. Some people say frontend is easier than backend. What do you think?
12. Why is frontend development important in a large enterprise system?
13. How do you explain your job to non-technical people (like your family or manager)?
14. What would happen to the product if there was no good frontend team?
15. How has your role changed in the last 5 years?
